Top 10 High-Speed Powerboats & Speedboats of 2026

Ranked by offshore performance, horsepower, and hull engineering

The high-performance powerboat world in 2026 is a paradox of extremes: 1,500-horsepower gasoline V8s pushing 50-foot hulls past 100 mph, and silent electric foiling boats that glide above the water like aircraft. From the legendary Cigarette Racing team that has dominated offshore racing for decades, to Swedish innovators at Candela who have redefined what's possible with electric marine propulsion, the top 10 list below spans the full spectrum of marine performance. Whether you crave the raw thunder of blown big-blocks or the whisper-quiet efficiency of hydrofoils, these are the most thrilling boats on water today.

  1. Cigarette Racing 515 Mk1

    Top Speed: 140+ mph Power: 3,100 hp Length: 51 ft Price: $2.4M

    The Cigarette 515 Mk1 is the ultimate expression of offshore racing DNA translated into a luxury day boat. Its twin Mercedes-Benz M8 quad-cam engines produce a combined 3,100 horsepower, propelling the 51-foot hull past 140 mph. Hand-laid carbon fiber and Kevlar composite construction, custom Llebroc seats, and a state-of-the-art Garmin helm make the 515 the most sought-after center console in the world.

  2. Candela C-8 Polestar Edition

    Top Speed: 50 mph Range: 57 mi Foiling: Yes Price: $415,000

    The Candela C-8 is the world's most advanced electric boat. Using computer-controlled hydrofoils, it lifts the hull above the water at 16 knots, eliminating 98% of drag and producing zero wake. The Polestar Edition, co-developed with Polestar Automotive, features a 69 kWh battery and a range of 57 nautical miles. It is silent, sustainable, and the most efficient boat per mile ever built.

  3. Scarab 195 Open ID

    Top Speed: 85 mph Engine: Supercharged 430 hp Length: 19 ft Price: $95,000

    The Scarab 195 Open ID is the most agile jet boat ever built, designed in collaboration with legendary offshore racer Donzi. Its supercharged Rotax 430 hp engine delivers instant acceleration and 85 mph top speed, while the JetDL steering system provides handling precision that traditional propeller boats cannot match. The 195 is the perfect combination of performance and family-friendly safety.

  4. Nor-Tech 450 Sport

    Top Speed: 120 mph Power: 1,800 hp Length: 45 ft Price: $1.8M

    The Nor-Tech 450 Sport is the most successful hull design in modern offshore racing. Its twin-step deep-V hull planes effortlessly in rough seas and has claimed more Class 1 and Superboat championships than any other. Available with twin, triple, or quad outboard configurations, the 450 delivers up to 1,800 horsepower of pure, reliable performance.

  5. Deep Impact 399 Center Console

    Top Speed: 92 mph Power: 1,400 hp Length: 39 ft Price: $1.1M

    The Deep Impact 399 is a luxury offshore fishing boat that doesn't sacrifice speed. Its quad 350 hp Mercury Verado outboards produce 1,400 horsepower, propelling the 39-foot hull to 92 mph. The 399 features a 360-degree fighting cockpit, a 75-gallon livewell, and the most advanced outboard rigging system in the industry.

  6. Skater 368 Catamaran

    Top Speed: 170 mph Power: 2,800 hp Length: 36 ft Price: $1.5M

    The Skater 368 is a purpose-built racing catamaran capable of speeds over 170 mph. Built by Douglas Marine in Michigan, it is the boat that has dominated American Powerboat Association (APBA) offshore racing for over a decade. Its carbon-fiber tunnel hull, twin Mercury Racing 1550 engines, and adjustable rudder system make it the most competitive race boat in its class.

  7. Sunsation 34 CC Xtreme

    Top Speed: 105 mph Power: 1,050 hp Length: 34 ft Price: $680,000

    The Sunsation 34 CC Xtreme is the highest-performance center console under 35 feet. Its twin 525 hp Mercury Racing engines produce 1,050 horsepower, delivering a 105 mph top speed in a package that can still comfortably cruise the Great Lakes or the Bahamas. Hand-crafted in Algonac, Michigan, every Sunsation is built to order.

  8. Princess R35

    Top Speed: 50 mph Engine: Twin diesel 1,050 hp Length: 35 ft Price: $1.4M

    The Princess R35 is a British luxury sport boat with racing DNA. Co-developed with BAR Technologies (the America's Cup design team), it uses an Active Foil System that automatically adjusts to lift the hull above the water at speed, dramatically improving efficiency and ride quality. The R35 is the most refined and innovative British sport boat ever produced.

  9. Axopar 28 Sun Top

    Top Speed: 47 mph Engine: Twin 200 hp outboards Length: 28 ft Price: $185,000

    The Axopar 28 Sun Top is the most successful Nordic sport boat of the decade. Its distinctive modular design, twin-step hull, and twin 200 hp Mercury outboards make it the perfect platform for day cruising, watersports, or adventure exploring. The Axopar 28 is as at home in Miami as it is in Monaco.

  10. Williams SportJet 460

    Top Speed: 60 mph Engine: Rotax 4-TEC 230 hp Length: 15 ft Price: $48,000

    The Williams SportJet 460 is the most desirable superyacht tender in the world. Its jet propulsion eliminates the exposed propeller, making it safe for swimmers, and the Rotax 4-TEC engine delivers 60 mph performance in a 15-foot package. Over 80% of the world's 100 largest yachts carry a Williams tender.

Final Thoughts

Whether you measure by speed, innovation, or luxury, 2026 is an extraordinary year for high-performance boating. The Cigarette 515 Mk1 still rules the raw-power category, but electric foiling boats from Candela and Princess are proving that the future of marine propulsion is silent, efficient, and increasingly luxurious. Whether you're carving wakes at 140 mph or gliding above the water on hydrofoils, these ten boats represent the absolute pinnacle of what humans can achieve on water. The horizon has never looked faster — or cleaner.
