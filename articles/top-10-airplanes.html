Top 10 Airplanes & Private Jets of 2026

Ranked by aerospace engineering, range, speed, and luxury

Aviation in 2026 is witnessing a golden age. From supersonic business jets that shrink transatlantic travel to just over three hours, to ultra-long-range private jets that connect virtually any two cities on Earth nonstop, the choices for discerning travelers have never been more impressive. Here is our ranking of the top 10 airplanes and private jets defining modern aviation.

  1. Bombardier Global 8000

    Range: 8,000 NM Speed: Mach 0.94 Passengers: 19 Price: $78M

    The Bombardier Global 8000 is the fastest purpose-built business jet in the world, with a top speed of Mach 0.94 that makes it nearly transonic. It also boasts an 8,000-nautical-mile range, enough to connect Los Angeles to Sydney or London to Perth nonstop. The innovative Nuage seat provides unmatched comfort on long-haul flights, and the cabin features Bombardier's patented Pur Air system that refreshes cabin air every 90 seconds.

  2. Gulfstream G800

    Range: 8,000 NM Speed: Mach 0.925 Passengers: 19 Price: $72M

    Gulfstream's flagship G800 combines the longest range in the industry with Gulfstream's signature high-speed performance. It features the revolutionary Symmetry Flight Deck with active control sidesticks and the most advanced touchscreen avionics in business aviation. The cabin offers 20 panoramic windows, a crew rest area, and a master suite with a shower.

  3. Dassault Falcon 10X

    Range: 7,500 NM Speed: Mach 0.925 Passengers: 19 Price: $70M

    The Falcon 10X is Dassault's largest and most advanced business jet, featuring a 6-foot-8-inch cabin height that allows passengers to walk upright. Its ultra-efficient wing design is derived from Dassault's fighter jet experience, and the fly-by-wire controls provide exceptional handling. The cabin is configurable with a full-size shower, a lounge, and a private office.

  4. Boom Supersonic Overture

    Range: 4,250 NM Speed: Mach 1.7 Passengers: 80 Status: In development

    The Overture is the most anticipated commercial aircraft since the Concorde. Designed to fly at Mach 1.7 with a range of 4,250 nautical miles, it will cut flight times in half on routes like New York to London (3.5 hours) and Tokyo to Seattle (4.5 hours). It runs on 100% sustainable aviation fuel and features a revolutionary symphony engine. First deliveries are expected by 2029.

  5. Embraer Praetor 600

    Range: 4,018 NM Speed: Mach 0.83 Passengers: 12 Price: $21M

    The Praetor 600 is the most disruptive super-midsize jet in the sky. It offers the longest range among its peers and the first full fly-by-wire in its class. Its Bossa Nova interior is designed for productivity and relaxation, featuring a vacuum toilet, a galley, and lie-flat seating for eight passengers. It can fly nonstop from London to Dubai or New York to Moscow.

  6. Cessna Citation Longitude

    Range: 3,500 NM Speed: Mach 0.84 Passengers: 12 Price: $29M

    The Citation Longitude offers exceptional value in the midsize-plus category. It features a fully flat-floor cabin, a stand-up cabin with 6 feet of headroom, and a pressurized baggage compartment. Its Garmin G5000 avionics suite includes a synthetic vision system and autothrottles, reducing pilot workload and enhancing safety.

  7. Airbus A350-1000ULR

    Range: 9,700 NM Speed: Mach 0.85 Passengers: 350 Price: $366M

    The A350-1000ULR is the ultimate commercial airliner, capable of flying farther than any other passenger aircraft. It powers the world's longest routes, including Singapore to New York — a 19-hour, 9,700-nautical-mile journey. The cabin features Airbus's latest Airspace interior with LED mood lighting, larger overhead bins, and whisper-quiet Rolls-Royce Trent XWB engines.

  8. Eve Air Mobility eVTOL

    Range: 60 NM Speed: 110 mph Passengers: 5 Status: Certification 2027

    Eve's electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft is designed to revolutionize urban mobility. With eight lift rotors plus a pusher prop for cruise, it's quiet, efficient, and zero-emission. It will carry four passengers and a pilot on urban air taxi routes, reducing commute times drastically. Eve already has over 2,000 orders from launch customers worldwide.

  9. Cirrus Vision Jet G2+

    Range: 1,275 NM Speed: 311 kts Passengers: 7 Price: $3.4M

    The Vision Jet is the world's best-selling personal jet, and the G2+ brings enhanced performance and the game-changing SafeReturn autoland system — if the pilot becomes incapacitated, the passenger simply pushes a red button and the aircraft lands itself autonomously at the nearest suitable airport. It's a single-pilot jet that makes private aviation accessible.

  10. ZeroAvia ZA2000 Hydrogen Aircraft

    Range: 500 NM Engine: Hydrogen-electric fuel cell Passengers: 80 Status: Prototype testing

    The ZA2000 represents the future of zero-emission aviation. Using hydrogen fuel cells to power electric motors, it produces only water vapor as a byproduct. ZeroAvia plans to certify the ZA2000 by 2027 for regional aircraft, and the technology promises to scale to larger aircraft by the early 2030s. It's a glimpse of a cleaner aviation future.

Final Thoughts

Whether you're looking for the ultimate business jet for global travel or following the development of technologies that will redefine flight itself, 2026 is an extraordinary time for aviation. The Bombardier Global 8000 takes our top spot for its unmatched combination of speed and range, but keep an eye on Boom's Overture — it's about to make the world smaller than ever before.
